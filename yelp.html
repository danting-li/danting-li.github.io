Yelp Redesign
Date: Fall 2019, Time: 1 Month,
Role: UX Design, Team: Personal Project.
Introduction
I love this quote from Sherlock Holmes: "You see, but you do not observe." I observe every app when I use it. I am a Yelp user and I've found out there to be a few usability problems with Yelp. I wanted to conduct UX research to establish empathy for users, approach the design from the angle of what users need, and solve any problems to enhance the user experience.
CLIENT BACKGROUND
Yelp is one of the most popular business directories worldwide; millions of users use it to discover and review local businesses and events. Yelp is facing a dilemma: Google has developed a similar product in direct competition with it. Yelp did much better in photo classification than Google. As a Yelp user, I really enjoy using Yelp over Google because I love going through images that have already been classified into food, drink, inside, outside and menu categories. Google uses image classification as well, but gets way too fancy with latest, food & drink, vibe, by owner, 360° view and videos features. Yelp also did a great job in having a popular dishes section to show the highlights of a particular restaurant's cuisine. If someone is new to this restaurant or just indecisive about a specific meal, the popular dishes section could be very helpful to them.
PROJECT SCOPE
I was approached with the task off redesigning the front-end of the Yelp app. The app should allow users to easily understand and navigate the app as well as provide a seamless experience when discovering and reviewing local businesses and events. I identified emerging trends, and turned them into reusable patterns, then applied system-provided interface elements, icons, text styles, and uniform terminology to innovative new features. I advocated for UI consistency. Simply put, I created a seamless, simple, instinctive, and memorable experience.
Research
I began the project by conducting semi-structured interviews with Yelp users in different industries to understand their search habits, restaurant choices, struggles, user behaviors, and so on. I also conducted a competitive analysis with products related to the user group such as Google Maps, OpenTable and others to understand the scope of existing solutions within this problem space.
Consumers often check Yelp. According to the 2018 Online Reviews Survey by ReviewTrackers, approximately 45% of consumers say they are likely to check reviews on Yelp before visiting a business. Yelp’s percentage is only second to Google (64%).
According to Yelp’s the factsheet, restaurants are their most-reviewed category.
Restaurants have waiting lists that total an average of 6.6 hours per week, with parties waiting nearly half an hour on average for a table, according to data from a new study. The average wait per party is 23 minutes, but nearly a third of all parties end up waiting more than 30 minutes. Eight percent wait longer than 40 minutes.
PAIN POINTS
From my research, I mainly realized that my interviewees:
  • Spend too much time in line and waiting during peak periods.
  • Find over-retouched photos different from their real-life experiences.
  • Find it difficult to share restaurant links to other social media platforms.
    • SYNTHESIS
    With my research learnings, I created two personas that embodied the archetypes of the user group. I mapped out the user journey of the persona Mark, including his thoughts and feelings during his typical weekday. From this, I was able to temporarily visualize Mark’s areas of frustrations and created a focal point on the problem space.
    DESIGN REQUIREMENTS
    Using the research and synthesis as guidelines, I summarized the findings with a problem statement for the user group: Yelp users want a more authentic, efficient and shareable platform.
    I identified a list of design requirements to address the problem statements. Most notably, the solution needs to:
  • Avoid peak hours and reduce waiting time in restaurants.
  • Be clearer and more detailed in photo classification.
  • Achieve pure user delight.
  • Improve the user experience of the sharing user flow.
    • Ideation
    With these design requirements in mind, I began to ideate on potential solutions through sketches and storyboards.
    INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE
    I went through the different possible designs that my solutions could take including video stories, popular times, etc. I ultimately decided to:
  • Create story highlights.
  • Add a video category in photo classification.
  • Add a popular times graph in the about page of the restaurant.
  • Redesign the share link interface.
    • I bucketed the design requirements as feature components of the information architecture (IA) to restructure Yelp. The texts circled by a red dashed line are the elements added or redesigned on Yelp.
    PAPER PROTOTYPE
    I then created Paper Prototypes based on the IA to quickly test general usability and to weed out confusing elements. This is conducted by requiring users to execute a list of instructions regarding several tasks with little to no help. Then, using the feedback from the usability test, I outlined the wireframes of the new version of Yelp and created several key path scenarios to further analyze the flow of interaction of the updated version of Yelp.
    WIREFRAME
    Task 1: Add video stories to the restaurant main page.
  • Reason: Videos are more mentally and emotionally sticky with viewers than still images.
  • Purpose: Videos about different user experieences of the restaurant provide more sensory input for deeper engagement.
    • Task 2: Add the video category in the photo classification page.
  • Reason: Videos reviews from users show in the video category of the photo classification. Video is more trustworthy, entertaining, relatable, accurate, informative, digestible and memorable. Videos are more reliable, engaging, informative and memorable than retouched images.
  • Purpose: Videos incite action. Videos prompt users to share reviews.
    • Task 3: Add popular times graph to the about page of the restaurant.
  • Reason: The popular times graph shows the peak hours of restaurants based on historical visits.
  • Purpose: Let users avoid spending too much time on line and waiting during peak periods for the restaurants.
    • Task 4: Redesign the interface of Yelp share link in WeChat.
  • Reason: The share function really matters to market every app, especially on Yelp. Chinese users are a big percentage of the Yelp users. The original design of the Yelp share link on WeChat has a bad user experience.
  • Purpose: Enhance the user flow by re-designing the UI and UX to let more Chinese Yelp users feel happy to share and use Yelp.
    • Design
    After several rounds of critiques on the wireframes, I moved on to high fidelity mockups. The motivation of the visual design was to create a bold fresh look that entices more users to use Yelp. I followed the current Yelp style guide and continued using Yelp's red color as the main theme.
  • Video Stories: A video collection customized by the owners to show highlighted stories about restaurants to the users.
  • Video Category: A video category is added to the photo classification. The videos are posted by the users.
  • Popular Times Graph: A graph shows the estimated wait time in a restaurant's about page.
  • Share Link: A redesign of the user interface of Yelp share link in WeChat.
    • Redesign
    I conducted the in-person usability tests to learn how users interact with the new design of Yelp and gather the feedback to identify usability issues.
  • How will I know if I have watched this video story?
  • Can you show what the video category page looks like?
  • If the waiting time is too long, how can it display on the graph?
  • Can it only take one step to go to a restaurant's Yelp page by clicking the Yelp share link in WeChat?
    • According to the feedback, I decided to improve the visuals, information hierarchy, consistency, and overall experience of the app by redesigning it.
  • Video Stories: Redesigned following the Yelp style guide to show the difference between viewed and not viewed to let users know which video stories they have viewed.
  • Video Category: Added the video icon to every video block to let users know they are in the Video Category of the photo classification page and that they can click to watch videos.
  • Popular Times Graph: The units on the Y-Axis are 30 minutes, one hour, and unlimited time. If the waiting time is longer than 1 hour, the time range of more than one hour in the bar is surrounded by a dashed line. The seven dots under the bar graph stand for seven days in a week.
  • Share Link: Users can click on the Yelp share link in WeChat and directly go to the restaurant page on Yelp.
    • Strategy
    SOUL
    You deserve a really good meal.
    MISSION
  • We are engaging. We want you to know our restaurants in a three-dimensional way.
  • We are authentic. We want to offer you more trustworthy reviews.
  • We are accurate. We show live wait times and want to save your time on line.
  • We are convenient. We enhance the user experience of the Yelp sharing link in WeChat.
    • BRAND KEYWORDS
    Authentic, factual, clear, consistent, accurate, convenient, engaging.
    Reflection
    In terms of this project, I realized that user interactions on the app are significant. In terms of the design process, I learned that I shouldn’t get caught up in the aesthetic details during the ideation and prototyping phase. It is important to quickly and iteratively test the usability and flow of interaction, correct any issues, then move on to the aesthetics at the end.
